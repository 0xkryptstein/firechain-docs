# Avoid Scams

Scams in Web3 often rely on urgency, fake links, fake addresses, and impersonation.

---

## Common Scam Patterns

Be cautious of:

- Fake bridge websites
- Fake token contracts
- Fake wallet support staff
- Fraudulent airdrops
- “Emergency” migration claims
- Requests for your recovery phrase or private key

---

## Safety Rules

- Never share your seed phrase
- Never share your private key
- Double-check every URL
- Double-check every contract address
- Use official docs as your source of truth
- Test with a small amount first

---

## Red Flag Examples

If someone says:

- “Use this new contract address immediately”
- “Connect your wallet to claim support funds”
- “Send tokens to verify your account”
- “Import this wallet for assistance”

do not proceed.
